Undergraduate Research

Data Analysis

2015 - Present
Muhlenberg College, PA

Analyzing data collected during summers 2015 and 2016 by completing area analysis in ImageJ, calculating averages, standard deviation, and preparing graphs in Excel, in addition to reading, interpreting, and writing primary literature. This work has culminated into an archived paper at the University of Washington and multiple research posters and talks.

The Effects of Reduced Salinity on the Green Sea Urchin (Lytechinus variegatus)

Fall 2016
Muhlenberg College, PA

Investigated the effects of reduced salinity on the green sea urchin (Lytechinus variegatus) under a scanning electron microscope. This research has been presented in a college-wide poster session.

Feeding Preferences of Freshwater Isopods (Lirceus sp.)

Spring 2015
Muhlenberg College, PA

Conducted experiments in a laboratory setting to determine whether freshwater isopods (Lirceus sp.) preferred fine-particulate organic material or coarse-particulate organic material as a habitat. This research included collecting isopods in the stream, maintaining the animals, and reading primary literature. Ultimately, this work culminated to a research poster at a regional conference.

Field Research

The Effects of Climate Change on Two Terrestrial Slugs (Ariolimax columbianus and Arion rufus)

Summer 2016
Friday Harbor Labs, WA

Independent researcher, advised by Erika V. Iyengar. This work, regarding the effects of climate change on two terrestrial slug species (Ariolimax columbianus and Arion rufus), will be incorporated into Biology Honors Thesis. This research has also resulted in multiple poster sessions.

The Competitive Relationship Between Two Terrestrial Slugs Ariolimax columbianus and Arion rufus

Summer 2015
Friday Harbor Labs, WA

Nation Science Foundation awarded position (Research Experience for Undergraduates), guided by Erika V. Iyengar. This research explored whether there was a competitive relationship between two terrestrial slug species (Ariolimax columbianus and Arion rufus) by analyzing feeding habits. This work culminated into multiple poster sessions and research talks.
